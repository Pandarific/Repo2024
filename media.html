Did you know?

  • Started with a Short Film: Shinkai’s first significant work, She and Her Cat (1999), was a five-minute short film he created entirely on his own. It won several awards, including the grand prize at the 12th DoGA CG Animation Contest in 2000.

  • His Love for Nature Shows in His Work: Shinkai grew up in Nagano Prefecture, surrounded by mountains and lush landscapes, which heavily influence the detailed natural settings in his films, such as The Garden of Words and Your Name.

  • Inspired by Western Literature: Shinkai has cited literature like Haruki Murakami’s novels and Western classics such as The Catcher in the Rye as sources of inspiration for the themes of isolation and self-discovery in his films.

  • His Films Often Feature Trains: Trains appear frequently in Shinkai’s films, symbolizing movement, connections, and the passage of time. This motif can be seen in 5 Centimeters per Second and Your Name.
